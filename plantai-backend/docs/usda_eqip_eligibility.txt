USDA EQIP — Environmental Quality Incentives Program: Eligibility, Application, and Funding

The Environmental Quality Incentives Program (EQIP) is administered by the USDA Natural Resources Conservation Service (NRCS) and provides financial and technical assistance to farmers and ranchers who implement conservation practices on working agricultural land. EQIP is one of the largest conservation programs in the United States, with annual funding exceeding $2 billion nationally.

Who Is Eligible for EQIP?

Any individual, legal entity, joint operation, or Indian tribe that is engaged in agricultural production may apply for EQIP. Eligible land includes cropland, grassland, rangeland, pastureland, non-industrial private forestland, and other farm or ranch land. To qualify, applicants must: (1) be the owner, operator, or tenant of eligible agricultural land; (2) be in compliance with highly erodible land and wetland conservation provisions; (3) not be debarred or suspended from federal programs; and (4) have an adjusted gross income (AGI) of $900,000 or less in the preceding 3 tax years, with exceptions for specialty crop and organic operations.

First-time farmers and beginning farmers (farming fewer than 10 years) receive priority ranking in EQIP. Historically underserved farmers also receive priority consideration and may receive a 25% payment rate increase.

What Practices Does EQIP Fund?

EQIP funds hundreds of conservation practices. Most relevant to small vegetable and row crop farms in Pennsylvania include:

- Cover crops (Practice 340): $30–$65/acre depending on species mix and application method
- Nutrient management plans (Practice 590): $5–$25/acre for plan development
- Irrigation system improvement (Practice 441, 442): $400–$2,500/acre for drip or subsurface systems
- Pest management (Practice 595): $8–$15/acre for integrated pest management planning
- High tunnel construction (Practice 325): $3–$8/linear foot or up to $15,000 per structure
- Soil testing and sampling (Practice 352): $3–$8/sample
- Conservation cover/pollinator habitat (Practice 327): $200–$450/acre establishment

Payment Rates and Funding Amounts

EQIP payments are based on practice-specific payment schedules established by each state NRCS office. Pennsylvania EQIP payment rates are updated annually. In Pennsylvania, small vegetable farms typically receive $3,000–$20,000 per contract, with contracts ranging from 1 to 10 years. Specialty crop and organic operations may receive up to $20,000/year. Organic transition projects may receive up to $80,000 over a 3-year contract. A single EQIP contract cannot exceed $450,000 over the life of the contract.

Application Process

1. Contact your local NRCS office to schedule a farm visit. Find your local office at offices.usda.gov. For Centre County PA, contact the State College NRCS Service Center.
2. Attend a preliminary assessment — an NRCS conservationist will visit your farm to identify resource concerns (soil erosion, water quality, nutrient loss, etc.).
3. Complete Form NRCS-CPA-1200 (EQIP Application for Payment) with your NRCS contact.
4. Develop a conservation plan identifying specific practices to implement.
5. Applications are ranked using a Resource Concern Priority Index. Applications are accepted year-round but reviewed in ranking periods, typically twice per year.
6. If funded, sign a contract and receive a payment schedule. Payments are made upon practice implementation and NRCS verification.

Timeline and Tips for Approval

Applications submitted in fall (September–November) align with NRCS's primary funding cycle. Document all existing conservation challenges with photos and soil test results — applications with clear resource concerns score higher. Combining multiple practices in one application increases total funding. Enroll in USDA's farm program records (FSA farm number) before applying; this is required. Beginning farmers should explicitly state their beginning farmer status in the application narrative.
